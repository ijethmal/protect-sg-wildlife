A Guide to Local Wild Boars You May Encounter

The "do"s, "don't"s, and everything in between



Wild boars are native animals of Singapore which can weigh up to 100kg and can live up to 20 years. They feed mainly on seeds, tubers, plants and flesh (omnivorous). At 18 months of age, a female wild boar can begin reproducing and produce 4 to 6 piglets per year.

As wild boar population increases, they may be more likely to roam into parks, public roadways, and residential areas, increasing human-wild boar conflict. Despite their meek appearance, they are still wild animals with unpredictable behavior that could endanger public safety.

What to do when you encounter a wild boar?

  • 1. Stay Calm and Move Slowly away from the wild boar
  • 2. Do not feed the wild boars
  • 3. Maintain a distance and do not provoke the wild boar
  • 4. Do not go near the young piglets as the adult wild boar will be more aggressive as they would want to defend their young.
