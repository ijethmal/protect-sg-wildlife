A Guide to Local Snakes You May Encounter

They may be slippery, but not so scary!



Fun fact a study in 2002 by NUS found that the snake assemblage at Pasir Ris (220 snakes collected) was dominated by Cerberus rynchops (72.7% of total snakes).

Cerberus rynchops also known as “dog faced water snake”. It is said that it got its common name for its protruding eyes, which is rather unusual for a snake. The snakes are docile and they are mildly venomous, having little to no effect on humans.

What to do when you encounter a snake?

  • 1. Stay Calm and Move Slowly away from the snake
  • 2. Do not feed the snake
  • 3. Maintain a distance and do not provoke the snake
